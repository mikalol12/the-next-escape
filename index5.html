Timeless Marvels:

Nestled on the shores of the Gulf of Tunis, Carthage stands as a testament to the ancient world's grandeur. Founded by the Phoenicians in the 9th century BC, this maritime empire flourished until its dramatic fall to Rome in 146 BC.Explore the remnants of Punic Ports, Byrsa Hill, and other structures that echo the strategic brilliance and architectural mastery of a bygone era.

Medina Mosaic:

Step into the heart of Tunis, where the UNESCO-listed Medina weaves a rich tapestry of history and culture. Established in the 9th century, this labyrinthine network of narrow streets and bustling markets reflects the city's vibrant past.

Phoenician Legacy:

Carthage's foundation is rooted in Phoenician ingenuity and maritime prowess. The city became a major trade hub, showcasing not only economic prosperity but also a unique blend of cultures. The ruins bear witness to the skillful architects and builders who constructed monuments that stood as a testament to the Phoenician identity and legacy.

Medina's Tapestry:

As centuries unfolded, the Medina of Tunis evolved through Islamic conquests and dynastic rule. Each era left its indelible mark on the city's architecture and culture. Today, the Medina remains a vibrant center, blending traditional charm with the dynamism of contemporary life. Its narrow alleys and historic sites continue to narrate a story of resilience, cultural exchange, and the enduring spirit of Tunis.
